Markings on side. Markings on centre hairline. Markings on additional hairlines. Slide reversed. Other side.

This Nestler does not exist physically. Inspired by FanHsiu's findings about the Hemmi 120 it assembles ideas from Faber 1/98 and Nestler 36, Darmstadt Faber 1/54 and Nestler 21 as well as from Hemmi 120 into a new system for sliderule scales.

A, B, CI, C, D as well as Sin and Tan are used as usual on the System Rietz, but divided in deci degs. The Sin and Tan side of the Slide features a C scale, so that it is very convenient to work withh the flipped slide. Contrary to the Darmstadt system chain calculations with Sin and Tan are possible. Small angles are handled by the C scale by means of 180/PI.

The Sinh scales allow to calculate Cosh by means of Cosh(x) = sqrt(1+Sinh(x)^2) using scale A. It also allows to calculate Tanh by the Gudermanian approach Tanh(x) = sin(tan^-1(Sinh(x)). Of course the slide needs to be flipped for this.
