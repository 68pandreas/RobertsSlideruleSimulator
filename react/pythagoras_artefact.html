Pythagoras artefact

You will now continue your trials learning from Pythagoras itself. It is not known how he got into posession of this artefact. Most likely he constructed it himself. It is, however, quite possible that he has seen an Egyptian artefact in action or perhaps even the one, which belonged to Hiram Abif himself.


Pythagoras' artefact, although similar to the Egyptian, includes several more scales. Their purpose is to perform reciprocal and trigonometrical calculations. It also has two additional green hairlines, which purpose will be explained during the course of the trials. The artefact is made of olive tree.

You will now be trained on how to use the artefact to multiply and divide using reciprocal scale as well as calculate trigonometrical functions. Understanding of trigonometry is not compulsory at this moment. All you need is to be able to use the artefact to obtain requested values. However, there are several useful relations, which you would find helpful:
 tan α = a / b  sin α = a / c  c = a / sin α  a = c × sin α
Good luck!
