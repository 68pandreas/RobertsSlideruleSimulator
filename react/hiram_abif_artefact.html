Hiram Abif's artefact

This is it! Congratulations! You have proven yourself to be worthy of discovering Hiram Abif's Artefact.


Hiram Abif was the chief architect of King Solomon's Temple. He was murdered in the Temple he designed by three ruffians during an unsuccessful attempt to force him to divulge the secrets of his craft.

In 1119 Knight Templars discovered the artefact, which Hiram Abif used for performing calculations in his design and construction work. Soon after this discovery, Gothic style in architecture was introduced in Europe. In 20th century similar tools were mass produced (named "slide rule") by companies such as Faber-Castell in Germany, Keufell & Esser in US and Hemmi in Japan. They were used for engineering purposes.

Ever since electronic calculators were invented, the art of using slide rules become forgotten.

You will now be introduced to several spectacular formulas and how they can be performed on the artefact. Enjoy!

Good luck!
