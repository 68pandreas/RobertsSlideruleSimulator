But what happened to Knight Templars?


On Friday 13th October 1307 the entire order of Knight Templars was arrested. About 3,000 knights and personnel were imprisoned. What happened to the remaining 12,000? They knew very well what was going to happen. One day before arrests 18 templar ships departed from French sea port La Rochelle. They were never seen again....

On 24th June 1314 at Bannockburn, Scottish king Robert the Bruce with 500 horsemen and 6,000 foot soldiers defeated the largest English army ever to invade Scottland, consisting of 2,000 heavily armoured knights and 16,000 infantry. This spectacular victory earned Scotland its independence. Nobody seem to remember the charge of "mystery" white knights, which caused king Edward II with his army to flee in fear and panic....

On 15th November 1315 at Morgarten a group of Swiss Confederates consisting of 1,500 peasants and shepherds defeated a 9,000 strong army of Leopold I, Duke of Austria. 2000 Austrian knights were killed while only 12 men died on the Swiss side. This victory firmly placed Switzerland on the map of Europe. Again nobody seem to remember those "mystery" fierce white knights.
The Swiss Confederation began 24 years before this battle. On the 1st August 1291 the Federal Charter was signed by just 3 little swiss regions. This happened almost 3 months after Knight Templars lost the city of Akka in the Holy Land on the 18th May of the same year. After this loss they needed to create a sovereign state somewhere else, where they could carry on their enterprises in peace....

Denis I, king of Portugal, refused to pursue and prosecute Knight Templars. Instead he renamed them as Order of Christ. With subsequent acceptance of Vatican, the knights began exploration of the oceans. In 1485, Christophe Columbus presented plans of his voyages to King John II of Portugal estimating the distance to India to be merely 2,400 miles. By that time Knight Templars already knew that there are two more continents to the west of Europe and Christopher Columbus together with his fantasies was sent away to look for sponsors someplace else. Nevertheless, when he finally sailed to the west under Spanish protectorate, his ships had templar crosses painted on sails....

Hiram Abif was the chief architect of King Solomon's Temple. He was murdered in the Temple he designed by three ruffians during an unsuccessful attempt to force him to divulge the secrets of his craft.
In 1119 Knight Templars discovered the artefact, which Hiram Abif used for performing calculations for his design and construction work.
