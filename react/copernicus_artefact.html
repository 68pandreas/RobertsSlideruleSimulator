Nicolaus Copernicus' artefact

Born in 1473 Nicolaus Copernicus was a man of renaissance. He described his "Heliocentric Theory" in his seminal work "De revolutionibus orbium coelestium", which was first printed in 1543 in Nuremberg. The scientific nature of his work together with use of latin made it difficult for the Holy Roman Church to react quickly. Once the Inquisition was ready to act and prosecute Copernicus for heresy, it was discovered that he already died peace in 1543 from natural causes and old age.


The artefact, which he used for calculations, was made from semi-transparent materials, which allowed him to watch the sky and perform calculations at the same time. You will now be trained and tested in a much more elaborate application of his artefact than before.
